Welcome to the BYOC Homepage!

The BYOC is scheduled for April 12th, 2014

Are you ready for one of the largest gaming events in Pennsylvania? The Penn College ACM and Gamers Guild BYOC LAN and Gaming Event has been a hit year after year, bringing in over 400 guests for 12 hours of gaming, friends, prizes and fun. All for FREE! This year we have a futuristic rouge science theme. So, be ready to fight off some intelligent AI and mad scientists in lab coats. Invite your friends! This event is FREE and open to the public!

What to bring:

Stuff you should bring if you plan to play games:

  • A surge protector - we only offer each player one plug.
  • 6' or longer Ethernet cord (We also supply some)
  • Your Desktop or Laptop
  • Gaming Consoles
  • Headphones - do not bring speakers! Speakers are way more likely to disturb your neighbors.
  • Monitor - TV monitors are allowed, but only if space does not become an issue. If it is an issue, we may ask you to relocate or use a smaller monitor. (This has never happened, but be forewarned)
  • A chair - optional. We do have folding chairs for everyone to use, but if you prefer a rolling chair/more comfortable chair, feel free to bring one, as long as it does not take up too much space. (again, hasn't been an issue before)

Stuff everyone should bring:

  • Money for food, t-shirts, and drinks.
  • Student ID - if you are a current Penn College student, you MUST bring a current student ID. (This lets you partake in the student-only door prizes)

Stuff you can bring if you want to:

  • You may bring any controller you wish to use for free-play, but there are NO third-party or modded controllers allowed for tournaments. Tourney masters have ultimate decision.
  • If you have a specific console game you would like to play, please bring it!
  • You may also bring your own console. We will have a secure area for console storage, but ultimately you are responsible for your own console!
  • We also offer a small number of projectors available for personal consoles that wish to use them. (We would prefer that these be used for cooperative or multiplayer games)
